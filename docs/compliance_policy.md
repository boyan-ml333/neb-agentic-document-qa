# Northeast Bank Consumer Lending Compliance Policy

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2025
**Policy Owner:** Chief Compliance Officer
**Review Cycle:** Annual

---

## 1. Purpose and Scope

This policy establishes the minimum compliance requirements for consumer lending activities conducted by Northeast Bank (the "Bank"). It applies to all loan origination, servicing, and collection activities for personal, home improvement, and debt consolidation loan products offered to retail customers.

The Bank is subject to federal consumer protection laws enforced by the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB) and applicable state banking regulations. Failure to comply may result in regulatory sanctions, civil liability, and reputational harm.

---

## 2. Truth in Lending Act (TILA) Requirements

### 2.1 Required Disclosures

In accordance with the Truth in Lending Act (15 U.S.C. § 1601 et seq.) and Regulation Z (12 C.F.R. Part 1026), the Bank must provide borrowers with the following disclosures **before** loan consummation:

- **Annual Percentage Rate (APR)**: The total cost of credit expressed as a yearly rate, inclusive of the interest rate and any fees incorporated into the finance charge.
- **Finance Charge**: The total dollar amount the loan will cost over its term.
- **Amount Financed**: The principal amount disbursed to the borrower (net of any prepaid finance charges such as origination fees).
- **Total of Payments**: The sum of all scheduled payments over the loan term.
- **Payment Schedule**: The number, timing, and amounts of each scheduled payment.

### 2.2 Right of Rescission

For loans secured by the borrower's primary dwelling (applicable to certain Home Improvement Loans), borrowers have the right to rescind the transaction within **three business days** of loan consummation or delivery of the rescission notice, whichever is later.

### 2.3 Advertising Standards

All marketing materials that reference a specific rate must also disclose the APR with equal prominence. Teaser rates that apply for less than one year must be identified as such.

## 3. Equal Credit Opportunity Act (ECOA) and Fair Lending

### 3.1 Non-Discrimination Requirements

Under the Equal Credit Opportunity Act (15 U.S.C. § 1691) and Regulation B (12 C.F.R. Part 1002), the Bank must not discriminate in any aspect of a credit transaction on the basis of:

- Race or color
- Religion
- National origin
- Sex (including gender identity and sexual orientation)
- Marital status
- Age (provided the applicant has the capacity to contract)
- Receipt of income from public assistance programs
- Good-faith exercise of rights under the Consumer Credit Protection Act

### 3.2 Adverse Action Notices

When an application is denied or approved on terms less favorable than those applied for, the Bank must provide the applicant with a written **adverse action notice** within **30 calendar days** of the decision. The notice must state the specific reasons for the adverse action or inform the applicant of the right to request those reasons.

### 3.3 Fair Lending Monitoring

The Compliance Department conducts quarterly statistical analyses of lending decisions to detect potential disparate impact. Significant disparities trigger a root-cause review. Results are reported to the Board Risk Committee annually.

## 4. Fair Debt Collection Practices Act (FDCPA)

Although the FDCPA primarily governs third-party debt collectors, the Bank applies equivalent standards to its internal collections activities:

- Borrowers may not be contacted before 8:00 a.m. or after 9:00 p.m. local time.
- Contact at the borrower's workplace is prohibited when the Bank knows the employer disapproves.
- Borrowers who provide written notice that they wish to cease communication must be contacted only to confirm no further contact or to notify of a specific action (e.g., referral to legal counsel).
- No false, deceptive, or misleading representations may be used in collections.

---

## 5. Community Reinvestment Act (CRA)

Northeast Bank is evaluated under the Community Reinvestment Act (12 U.S.C. § 2901) for its record of meeting the credit needs of its entire assessment area, including low- and moderate-income (LMI) geographies and borrowers. The Bank's CRA plan includes:

- Targeted outreach to LMI communities for personal loan products.
- Participation in Small Business Administration (SBA) lending programs.
- Annual contribution to CDFI-certified organizations in its assessment area.

## 6. Data Privacy and Information Security

### 6.1 Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act (GLBA)

The Bank provides borrowers with a privacy notice at the time of account opening, explaining what nonpublic personal information (NPI) is collected, how it is shared, and the borrower's right to opt out of certain sharing. NPI includes Social Security numbers, account numbers, income, and credit history.

### 6.2 Data Retention

Loan application records, credit reports, adverse action notices, and signed disclosures must be retained for a minimum of **25 months** from the date of application. Signed loan agreements must be retained for the life of the loan plus **7 years**.

### 6.3 Data Breach Response

In the event of unauthorized access to borrower NPI, the Bank's Incident Response Team must notify affected borrowers within **72 hours** of confirmed breach discovery, consistent with applicable state notification laws. The CFPB and prudential regulator are notified as required by supervisory guidance.

## 7. Subprime Lending Safeguards

Loans originated to borrowers with FICO scores below 620 are classified as **subprime** and subject to additional controls:

- Enhanced ability-to-repay assessment: debt-to-income (DTI) ratio must not exceed 43% post-origination.
- Mandatory verbal disclosure of APR and total finance charge at the time of application.
- Second-level manager review and approval required for any exception to standard underwriting criteria.
- Quarterly portfolio review by the Credit Risk Committee for concentration and performance monitoring.
- Prohibited features on subprime loans: balloon payments, negative amortization, prepayment penalties, and mandatory arbitration clauses.

---

## 8. Military Lending Act (MLA)

The Bank extends the following protections to active-duty servicemembers and their dependents:

- The Military Annual Percentage Rate (MAPR) — which includes most fees — is capped at **36%**.
- Mandatory arbitration clauses and waivers of the right to legal recourse are prohibited.
- Before originating a covered loan, the Bank verifies MLA-covered status using the DoD's MLA database.

---

## 9. Complaints Management

All consumer complaints related to lending products must be:

1. Logged in the Bank's complaint tracking system within **one business day** of receipt.
2. Acknowledged to the complainant within **three business days**.
3. Resolved and closed within **45 calendar days**, with a written response to the complainant.
4. Reported in aggregate to the Board Risk Committee quarterly.

Complaints alleging discrimination or UDAP (unfair, deceptive, or abusive acts or practices) are escalated immediately to the Compliance Department and General Counsel.

---

## 10. Policy Violations and Disciplinary Action

Employees who knowingly violate this policy are subject to disciplinary action up to and including termination. Violations that result in regulatory sanctions or customer harm may be referred to appropriate law enforcement authorities.
