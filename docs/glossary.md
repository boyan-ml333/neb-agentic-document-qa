# Banking and Lending Glossary

A reference for common terms used in Northeast Bank loan documents and communications.

---

## A

### Amortization

The process of spreading loan payments over time so that each payment covers both accrued interest and a portion of the principal. In a fully amortizing loan, the balance reaches zero at the end of the scheduled term. Early payments in an amortization schedule are weighted more heavily toward interest; later payments shift toward principal.

**Formula (fixed-rate):**
Monthly Payment = P × r(1+r)^n / ((1+r)^n − 1)
where P = principal, r = monthly interest rate (APR ÷ 12), n = number of monthly payments.

### Annual Percentage Rate (APR)

The yearly cost of borrowing expressed as a percentage, including the nominal interest rate and most fees (such as origination fees) that are required to obtain the loan. APR is mandated by the Truth in Lending Act (TILA) to allow consumers to compare loan offers on a standardized basis. A loan with a lower stated interest rate but high fees may have a higher APR than a loan with a higher stated rate and no fees.

### APY (Annual Percentage Yield)

The effective annual rate of return on a deposit account, accounting for compounding within the year. APY applies to savings and deposit products; APR applies to loans. APY is always equal to or greater than the stated nominal interest rate.

---

## B

### Balloon Payment

A large lump-sum payment due at the end of a loan term. Balloon loans typically have lower monthly payments during the term but require the borrower to pay off the remaining principal balance in full at maturity. Balloon payments are **prohibited** on Northeast Bank subprime loan products.

### Basis Point (bps)

One-hundredth of one percentage point (0.01%). Used to describe changes in interest rates or yield spreads. For example, an increase from 6.50% to 6.75% is a 25-basis-point increase.

---

## C

### Co-applicant (Co-borrower)

A second person who applies for a loan jointly with the primary borrower. Both parties are equally liable for repayment. The co-applicant's income, assets, and credit history are considered in underwriting. Distinct from a **guarantor**, who is only obligated to pay if the primary borrower defaults.

### Collateral

An asset pledged by a borrower to secure a loan. If the borrower defaults, the lender may seize and sell the collateral to recover the outstanding balance. Secured loans carry collateral; unsecured loans do not. Northeast Bank Standard Personal Loans and Debt Consolidation Loans are unsecured.

### Credit Bureau

An organization that collects, maintains, and sells credit information about consumers. The three major U.S. credit bureaus are Equifax, Experian, and TransUnion. Lenders report payment history, balances, and derogatory marks to these bureaus, which compile credit reports used to generate credit scores.

### Credit Utilization Ratio

The percentage of a borrower's available revolving credit (credit cards, lines of credit) that is currently in use. Calculated as: (total revolving balances) ÷ (total revolving credit limits). Lower utilization generally correlates with higher credit scores; utilization above 30% is commonly cited as a risk indicator.

---

## D

### Debt-to-Income Ratio (DTI)

A measure of a borrower's monthly debt obligations relative to gross monthly income.
**Formula:** DTI = (total monthly debt payments) ÷ (gross monthly income) × 100.
Northeast Bank requires a post-origination DTI of 43% or below for subprime borrowers. A DTI above 50% is generally a disqualifying factor across all loan products.

### Default

Failure to fulfill the legal obligations of a loan agreement. Common triggers include missing a specified number of consecutive payments (typically three) or filing for bankruptcy. Default may result in acceleration of the full outstanding balance, referral to collections, and negative credit bureau reporting.

### Disbursement

The release of loan funds to the borrower after loan consummation. Northeast Bank disburses to a Northeast Bank checking account within 24 hours of approval; external ACH disbursements take 2–3 business days.

---

## E

### Escrow

An arrangement in which a neutral third party holds funds until specified conditions are met. In mortgage lending, escrow accounts are commonly used to collect monthly portions of property taxes and homeowner's insurance, which the lender pays on the borrower's behalf when due. Not applicable to Northeast Bank personal loan products.

---

## F

### FICO Score

A credit score produced by Fair Isaac Corporation (FICO), ranging from 300 to 850. It is the most widely used credit scoring model in U.S. consumer lending. Higher scores indicate lower credit risk. Northeast Bank uses FICO scores as a primary underwriting input. Scores are derived from payment history (35%), amounts owed (30%), length of credit history (15%), new credit (10%), and credit mix (10%).

### Finance Charge

The total dollar cost of credit over the loan term, expressed as a dollar amount rather than a percentage. Under TILA, the finance charge includes interest, origination fees, and other required charges. It is disclosed on the Loan Estimate and Closing Disclosure.

---

## G

### Grace Period

A period after a payment due date during which a payment may be received without incurring a late fee. Northeast Bank's grace period is **10 calendar days**. The grace period does not prevent interest from accruing on the outstanding balance.

---

## H

### Hard Inquiry

A credit inquiry initiated by a lender when evaluating a credit application. Hard inquiries appear on the borrower's credit report and may temporarily lower the FICO score by a few points. Multiple hard inquiries within a short window (typically 14–45 days) for the same loan type may be treated as a single inquiry under FICO scoring models. Contrast with **soft inquiry**.

---

## L

### Loan-to-Value Ratio (LTV)

The ratio of the loan amount to the appraised value of the collateral asset. Used primarily in mortgage and secured lending.
**Formula:** LTV = loan amount ÷ appraised value × 100.
A lower LTV indicates greater borrower equity and lower lender risk. Not directly applicable to unsecured personal loans.

---

## O

### Origination Fee

A one-time fee charged by a lender to process and fund a new loan. Expressed as a percentage of the principal. At Northeast Bank, origination fees range from 0.5% to 3.0% and are deducted from the disbursed amount. The fee is included in the APR calculation.

---

## P

### Prepayment

Paying off all or part of a loan balance before the scheduled maturity date. Northeast Bank does not charge prepayment penalties, meaning borrowers may make additional principal payments or pay off the loan in full at any time without additional fees.

### Prepayment Penalty

A fee charged by some lenders when a borrower pays off a loan ahead of schedule. Prepayment penalties compensate the lender for lost interest income. **Northeast Bank does not charge prepayment penalties** on any personal loan product. Prepayment penalties are also prohibited on Northeast Bank subprime loan products per the Compliance Policy.

### Principal

The original amount of money borrowed, or the outstanding balance of the loan before interest is applied. Monthly amortization payments reduce the principal balance over time.

---

## S

### Soft Inquiry

A credit check that does not affect the borrower's credit score. Soft inquiries include pre-qualification checks, background checks by employers, and account reviews by existing creditors. Northeast Bank performs a soft inquiry when a borrower checks their rate online; a **hard inquiry** is performed only upon formal application submission.

### Subprime

A classification applied to borrowers with FICO scores below 620, indicating elevated credit risk. Subprime loans typically carry higher APRs to compensate for increased default probability. Northeast Bank's Compliance Policy imposes additional safeguards on subprime originations, including a 43% DTI cap, second-level approval, and prohibitions on balloon payments and prepayment penalties.

---

## T

### Term

The length of time over which a loan is scheduled to be repaid, expressed in months. Northeast Bank personal loans offer terms of 12 to 84 months. Longer terms result in lower monthly payments but higher total interest paid; shorter terms result in higher monthly payments but lower total cost.

### Truth in Lending Act (TILA)

Federal legislation (15 U.S.C. § 1601) requiring lenders to disclose the cost of credit in standardized terms, including APR, finance charge, amount financed, and total of payments. Implemented by Regulation Z. Designed to enable consumers to compare credit offers on an equal basis.

---

## U

### Underwriting

The process by which a lender evaluates a loan application to determine creditworthiness and set loan terms. Factors typically reviewed include credit score, income, employment history, DTI ratio, and existing debt obligations. Northeast Bank uses automated underwriting for standard applications, with manual review triggered by exception conditions or borderline risk profiles.

### Unsecured Loan

A loan not backed by collateral. The lender's recourse in the event of default is limited to legal action and credit reporting; the lender cannot directly seize assets without a court judgment. Northeast Bank's Standard Personal Loans and Debt Consolidation Loans are unsecured.
