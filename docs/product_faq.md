# Northeast Bank Personal Loan — Frequently Asked Questions

## General Information

### What types of personal loans does Northeast Bank offer?

Northeast Bank offers three personal loan products:

- **Standard Personal Loan**: Fixed-rate installment loans from $2,000 to $50,000, terms of 12–84 months.
- **Home Improvement Loan**: Secured or unsecured loans up to $100,000 for qualifying renovation projects.
- **Debt Consolidation Loan**: Designed to combine multiple high-interest debts into a single monthly payment, amounts up to $35,000.

### What credit score do I need to qualify?

A minimum FICO score of 620 is required for Standard and Debt Consolidation loans. Home Improvement Loans require a minimum score of 660 for the unsecured option. Applicants with scores above 750 typically receive our lowest advertised rates.

### How long does the application process take?

Most decisions are made within one business day of receiving a complete application. Funded disbursements to a Northeast Bank checking account are processed within 24 hours of loan approval. Disbursements to external accounts may take 2–3 business days via ACH.

### Can I apply jointly with another person?

Yes. Co-applicants are permitted. Both applicants' credit profiles are evaluated; the primary borrower is the party with the higher income. Co-applicants share equal liability for the outstanding balance.

## Interest Rates and Fees

### What interest rates does Northeast Bank charge?

Annual Percentage Rates (APRs) range from **6.99% to 24.99%** depending on creditworthiness, loan amount, and term length. The APR includes the interest rate and any applicable origination fee.

### Is there an origination fee?

An origination fee of **0.5%–3.0%** of the loan principal may apply. The fee is deducted from the disbursed amount; for example, a $10,000 loan with a 2% origination fee results in a net disbursement of $9,800 while your repayment obligation is based on the full $10,000 principal.

### Are there prepayment penalties?

No. Northeast Bank does not charge prepayment penalties on any personal loan product. Borrowers may pay off the balance in full or make additional principal payments at any time without incurring additional fees.

### What is the late payment fee?

A late payment fee of **$25** or **5% of the overdue installment** (whichever is greater) is assessed when a payment is received more than 10 calendar days past its due date.

## Repayment

### How are monthly payments calculated?

Monthly payments are calculated using the standard amortization formula based on the loan principal, the monthly interest rate (APR ÷ 12), and the number of monthly installments. Each payment covers accrued interest first; any remainder reduces the outstanding principal.

### Can I change my payment due date?

Borrowers may request a one-time payment due-date change within the first 60 days of the loan. To request a change, contact Northeast Bank Customer Service at 1-800-NEB-LOAN.

### What happens if I miss a payment?

If a payment is missed, Northeast Bank will attempt to notify the borrower by email and phone within two business days. A grace period of 10 calendar days applies before the late fee is assessed. Accounts 30 or more days past due are reported to the major credit bureaus. Accounts 90 or more days past due may be referred to collections.

### Is automatic payment (autopay) available?

Yes. Enrolling in autopay from a Northeast Bank checking account qualifies the borrower for a **0.25% APR discount** applied for the life of the loan. Autopay enrollment can be completed in the Northeast Bank mobile app or online portal.

## Loan Management

### How do I check my loan balance and payment history?

Loan details, including outstanding principal, next payment date, and full payment history, are available in the Northeast Bank online portal and mobile app 24/7.

### Can I increase my loan amount after origination?

No. Existing personal loans cannot be increased after origination. Borrowers who need additional funds may apply for a new loan, subject to standard underwriting. Outstanding loans are considered as existing debt obligations in the new application's debt-to-income calculation.

### What collateral is required?

Standard Personal Loans and Debt Consolidation Loans are **unsecured** — no collateral is required. The unsecured Home Improvement Loan is also unsecured up to $50,000. Amounts above $50,000 for Home Improvement require a lien on the property being improved.

## Eligibility and Documentation

### Who is eligible to apply?

Applicants must be:
- At least 18 years of age
- A U.S. citizen or permanent resident
- A resident of a state in which Northeast Bank operates
- Able to demonstrate a steady source of income

### What documents are required?

Standard documentation includes:
- Government-issued photo ID (driver's license or passport)
- Two most recent pay stubs or, for self-employed applicants, the two most recent federal tax returns (Form 1040 with all schedules)
- Bank statements for the most recent 60 days
- Proof of address (utility bill or lease agreement dated within 90 days)

### Does Northeast Bank conduct a hard credit inquiry?

Checking your rate with a soft inquiry does not affect your credit score. A hard inquiry is performed only after you submit a full application and consent to the credit check. The hard inquiry appears on your credit report and may temporarily lower your score by a few points.
